Two networks, one decision

The UAE has two major mobile operators: Etisalat (now branded e&) and Du (Emirates Integrated Telecommunications). Both run modern 4G and 5G networks and both sell postpaid plans from budget to ultra-premium. The differences that actually decide your monthly bill are in promotional pricing, coverage outside the city centres, international calling, and extras like free VIP numbers. Here is how they line up for 2026, tier by tier, using Etisalat's current promotional prices.

Network coverage: Etisalat is wider outside the cities

Etisalat was the UAE's first operator and the first to launch 5G, which shows up as a wider footprint once you leave central Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Where Du is the better pick (the honest bit)

No provider wins on everything. Du can be the smarter choice if you:

  • Live and work entirely in central Dubai or Abu Dhabi — where the coverage gap mostly disappears.
  • Want Du Entertainer / OSN-style bundles baked into your plan.
  • Are a short-stay resident who values contract flexibility over the widest coverage and the biggest promotional discounts.

For everyone else — anyone who travels between emirates, calls abroad, wants a free VIP number, or just wants the most data and minutes per dirham this year — Etisalat is the stronger value.
